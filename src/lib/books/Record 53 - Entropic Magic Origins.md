# Record 53: Entropic Magic Origins – Ley Flow Degradation Theory
> **Author:** Martin Ehrlich
> **Written:** 804 AD
## Source Material
Analysis based on direct observations made during the major Waygate ritual, combined with regional magical phenomena and historical correlations.
## Fundamental Theory: Magic's Natural State
The evidence suggests that entropic magic represents the natural, primordial state of all magical energy. The current system of eight distinct magical schools may be an artificial construct imposed upon what should naturally exist as unified, chaotic magical force.

During the Waygate observations, entropy was present as an integral component of the Ley rather than a byproduct. This indicates that what we consider "abnormal" may actually be magic attempting to return to its original form.
## Waygate Intervention Theory
The Waygates appear to serve a more fundamental purpose than simple flow acceleration. I theorize that they function as magical separation devices, artificially maintaining the division of entropic magic into the eight schools we currently utilize.

The accelerated flow beneath Waygates prevents entropic accumulation not because it eliminates waste, but because it maintains sufficient speed to sustain the artificial separation process. When flow slows, the magic begins reverting to its natural entropic state.
## Historical Implementation
At some point in the distant past, an organized group likely implemented the Waygate network specifically to transform the world's magical ecosystem. Rather than working with wild, chaotic entropic magic, they created a system that would separate this energy into controllable, specialized forms.

This would explain why both the Waygate-enhanced state and the entropic masses are described as unnatural by the ritual participants. Neither represents the true baseline of magical energy... one is artificially accelerated, the other is artificially stagnated.
## The Mortium Connection
The entropic mass beneath Mortium may sit directly where a Waygate once functioned. This is not coincidental. When that Waygate was destroyed, the magical energy in that region lost its artificial separation mechanism and began consolidating back into its natural entropic form.

The growing mass represents magic actively reclaiming its original state, extracting additional energy from the Ley to fuel this reversion process.
## Regional Degradation Pattern
The dozen compromised areas observed throughout the region likely represent weak points in the separation network. As the system degrades, more areas lose the energy necessary to maintain artificial school divisions.

The secondary formation in Farbane suggests this process is accelerating. Without intervention, the entire regional magical ecosystem may revert to pure entropic magic within a few years.
## Implications for Magical Practice
If this theory proves correct, our current understanding of magic is fundamentally flawed. What we consider "proper" magic may be entirely dependent on artificial infrastructure. Practitioners may have lost the knowledge of how to work with magic in its natural entropic state.

The organized schools of magic... Holy, Blood, Chaos, Unholy, Shadow, Frost, Storm, and Illusion... may represent nothing more than arbitrary divisions imposed on a unified force.
## Remediation Considerations
Simply repairing or strengthening Waygates would perpetuate an artificial system without addressing whether this approach serves our long-term interests. We must consider whether maintaining the current magical schools is worth the infrastructure burden.

Alternatively, research into working with entropic magic directly might provide more sustainable magical practices that do not require constant technological intervention.
