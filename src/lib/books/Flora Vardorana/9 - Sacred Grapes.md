# Sacred Grapes: *Vitis Sacra*
> **Series:** Flora Vardorana: A Comprehensive Study, Volume IX  
> **Author:** Verena Stillswater  
> **Written:** AD 59
## Description and Habitat
The sacred grape displays typical grape morphology: deep blue to purple berries growing in tight clusters. Close examination reveals subtle differences from common varieties - the fruit possesses slightly thicker skin and demonstrates unusual resilience to typical grape diseases. The vines grow vigorously, producing consistent yields.

Sacred grapes originated at Brighthaven Vineyards, where the Church of Luminance established extensive cultivation after settling the region. The variety now propagates reliably from seed, maintaining its unique properties regardless of growing location. The holy essence appears inherent to the plant lineage rather than dependent on environmental factors.

What distinguishes sacred grapes from common varieties lies not in appearance but in their unusual tolerance for holy magic and the trace amounts they contain within their flesh and seeds.
## Theories of Origin
Sacred grapes represent either intentional cultivation or gradual mutation of conventional grape varieties exposed to sustained holy magic. The Church of Luminance established Brighthaven Vineyards and blessed the grounds extensively, performing regular rituals and ceremonies among the vines. Over multiple generations of grape cultivation in this environment, the plants adapted to tolerate and eventually incorporate trace amounts of holy essence.

The mutation appears stable and heritable. Seeds collected from sacred grape vines produce offspring with identical properties regardless of growing conditions, suggesting the holy tolerance has become fixed in the plant's fundamental nature. Whether the Church deliberately bred for this trait or simply observed its spontaneous emergence remains uncertain, though historical records suggest surprise at the development rather than intent.

The sacred grape lineage demonstrates how sustained exposure to specific magical energies can permanently alter plant characteristics across generations.
## Harvesting and Preparation
Harvest when clusters reach full color and sugar content peaks. The grapes handle like any wine grape during harvest and processing. No special precautions required; the trace holy content proves insufficient to harm vampiric harvesters during brief handling.

Fresh grapes maintain optimal properties. Processing must occur relatively quickly, as the fruit deteriorates normally once separated from the vine. Standard wine-making techniques apply. Seeds collected from sacred grape vines reliably produce offspring with identical properties, allowing cultivation in any suitable grape-growing environment.
## Properties and Applications
Sacred grapes serve primarily in applications requiring controlled holy exposure. When combined with prisoner blood in sealed vessels, the grapes apparently produce blood merlot, a refined beverage reported to be favored in vampiric circles. Multiple accounts describe the trace holy content creating mild intoxicating effects for vampire consumers, functioning analogously to alcohol's effects on living beings. The holy essence apparently acts as a controlled toxin, producing desirable sensations in carefully managed quantities.

Alchemists employ sacred grapes in formulations designed to provide holy resistance. The mechanism appears related to gradual tolerance building through controlled exposure to trace amounts of holy essence. Regular consumption of such preparations reportedly increases resistance to stronger holy magic over time, though the efficacy and duration of this protection requires more extensive documentation.

Preparations combining sacred grapes with snow flower and appropriate reagents apparently enhance spell potency significantly. Practitioners suggest the holy trace somehow stabilizes or amplifies magical workings when properly incorporated, indicating complex interactions between holy and arcane energies that warrant deeper investigation into the relationship between these typically opposing forces.

Horse trainers incorporating sacred grapes into feed report improved agility and pivoting response in their charges. The mechanism behind this enhancement remains unexplained given the fruit's holy associations. Whether the effect derives from the holy trace, conventional nutritional content, or some entirely separate property represents an open question.
