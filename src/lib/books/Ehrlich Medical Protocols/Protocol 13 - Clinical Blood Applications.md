# Protocol 13: Clinical Blood Applications
> **Series:** Ehrlich Medical Protocols
> **Author:** Martin Ehrlich
> **Written:** 206 AMR

## Purpose
To document comprehensive clinical applications of blood type healing effects and combination therapies. Establishes systematic framework for blood type classification, individual healing properties, and synergistic treatment protocols. Provides clinical guidance for treatment selection based on injury type and patient condition.

## Blood Type Classification Framework
Blood type healing mechanisms operate through distinct physiological pathways. Primary effects determine specialization: endurance enhancement, regenerative acceleration, mobility improvement, structural repair, magical protection, genetic adaptation, shadow healing, and toxin resistance. Combination therapies leverage synergistic properties between blood types to enhance clinical outcomes.

## Clinical Applications by Condition

### Physical Trauma
**Severe Trauma:** Brute blood primary. Creature or Warrior combinations enhance tissue recovery.
**Bone Fractures:** Warrior blood essential. Creature or Brute combinations accelerate density restoration.
**Muscle Tears:** Warrior blood primary. Creature or Brute combinations support muscle mass restoration.
**Deep Wounds:** Creature blood essential. Brute or Warrior combinations enhance closure.

### Magical Injuries
**Spell Damage:** Scholar blood essential. Draculin combinations accelerate magical wound healing.
**Shadow Corruption:** Draculin blood essential. Corrupted combinations process concurrent corruption.
**Magical Burns:** Scholar blood primary. Draculin combinations provide aggressive magical healing.
**Curse Wounds:** Scholar blood essential. Corrupted combinations process magical toxins.

### Functional Impairment
**Mobility Restrictions:** Rogue blood essential. Worker or Creature combinations support sustained mobility.
**Fine Motor Dysfunction:** Rogue blood primary. Worker combinations enhance dexterity during recovery.
**Precision Loss:** Rogue blood essential. Scholar combinations maintain magical sensitivity.

### Environmental Exposure
**Toxin Exposure:** Corrupted blood essential. Mutant combinations adapt healing to toxin type.
**Chemical Poisoning:** Corrupted blood primary. Mutant combinations process genetic susceptibility factors.
**Corrupted Environment:** Corrupted blood essential. Scholar combinations maintain barrier protection.
**Harsh Conditions:** Mutant blood essential. Worker combinations support adaptation during recovery.

### Exhaustion and Fatigue
**Physical Exhaustion:** Worker blood essential. Creature combinations support natural regeneration.
**Repeated Strain:** Worker blood primary. Rogue combinations preserve precision during recovery.
**Extended Recovery:** Worker blood essential. Scholar combinations provide magical resistance.

## Contraindications & Considerations

### Absolute Contraindications
- **Shadow Energy Intolerance:** Avoid Draculin blood and all Draculin combinations.
- **Magical Sensitivity Disorders:** Exercise caution with Scholar blood and all Scholar combinations.
- **Genetic Instability:** Corrupted and Mutant combinations require extensive patient screening.

### Relative Contraindications
- **Active Infection:** Brute blood accelerates healing but may seal infection beneath tissue. Employ with sterile procedures.
- **Shadow Energy Sensitivity:** Draculin combinations effective but require careful dosage monitoring.
- **Toxin Processing Compromise:** Corrupted blood contraindicated when detoxification systems are overwhelmed.

### Clinical Considerations
- **Combination Timing:** Sequential application may prevent synergistic interference. Monitor patient response between blood type transitions.
- **Dosage Adjustment:** Patient mass, injury severity, and healing response inform dosage parameters.
- **Environmental Factors:** Corrupted and Mutant effectiveness varies based on environmental conditions and toxin presence.
- **Magical Load:** Scholar and Draculin efficacy influenced by ambient magical conditions and patient sensitivity.
