## A Note on Circumstances
The Academy is currently inaccessible to me, which is to say I am trapped in the Astrological Department by whatever disaster has sealed off the lower floors, and while I expect that someone will come to sort it out eventually, the waiting has given me an unusual abundance of time and no particular reason not to use it. Asmodeus spent the better part of three decades discouraging me from committing these theories to paper on the grounds that I would, and I'm paraphrasing his exact words here, embarrass the institution with speculation that outran my evidence. He was wrong about that, in my view, but he was the Director and I respected the constraint while he was present to enforce it. He is not present now, and the enchanted pen he badgered me into making has to be good for something, so I intend to document the full framework of the ladder positions while I wait for the rescue party that will surely arrive before too long, beginning with ♈. Mordecai's telescope is intact, which is fortunate, and his smaller instruments appear undamaged as well, so at minimum the observations I record here will be properly measured.
## The Burning Position
The first thing anyone needs to understand about Ash Forge is that it is not a process, and I feel compelled to lead with that because every alchemist I've spent any meaningful time with assumes burning is something you perform rather than something that happens to you. They add heat, introduce a flame source, coax some desired reaction out of reluctant material, and they call this burning, which is a description of labor rather than of transformation and has nothing whatsoever to do with ♈.

♈ is a position, a state the cosmos settles into when the geometry aligns correctly overhead, and when that geometry holds, burning happens not because anyone compelled it but because the position demands it of everything within range. Impurities that have survived every previous state become visible during the Ash Forge alignment, and recognizing themselves as incompatible with the pattern they inhabit, they remove themselves without any external force being applied, which is a meaningfully different thing from being destroyed by heat even if the observable result looks similar to someone not paying close attention.

I watched this in the old chemistry workshop across forty-seven alignment events. During ☉ at ♈ around the summer solstice, Malcolm's alchemical reagents would spontaneously ignite on the shelf without any proximate source of ignition, and he blamed unstable compounds every single time, which was understandable given that he had no framework for what was actually occurring. The reagents weren't unstable; their impurities could not survive the alignment and removed themselves, which manifested as what looked like spontaneous combustion but was something considerably more specific than that. Asmodeus at least found the observations interesting enough to write down.
## The Ash Forge Constellation ♈
Thirty-seven stars in the southeastern sky during summer months, forming a chimney structure with three bright stars at the hearth base, and the geometry is precise enough that I cannot accept the Academy's position that this is a random grouping, because the arrangement describes three hearth stars, a vertical chimney of stacked star-bricks, and the whole structure oriented exactly as a working forge would be oriented, which is not a configuration that emerges accidentally from the same processes that produce genuinely random star scatters. I've counted those stars forty-seven times since being trapped here and the number is always thirty-seven, which I am certain means something about the purification principle even if I haven't resolved exactly what it means yet, though I note that ♉ has twenty-three stars that connect cleanly to the compression principle, which suggests the star counts in these constellations are not arbitrary.

When ☉ at ♈ the hearth stars brighten noticeably enough to see without equipment, and the chimney structure seems to draw Theralen's light upward through it in a way that is either a remarkable natural alignment or the most intentional star arrangement I've ever documented, and I've documented rather a lot of them at this point. The meteorite shard shows a faint internal glow during these alignments, which I've noted across three summers now and find genuinely curious even if I can't yet determine what exactly is being purified at the shard's scale.
## What Burns and What Remains
What survives the Ash Forge position is the essential structure of the pattern without its contradictions, finally legible in a way it wasn't before, and this matters considerably for what comes next because ♉ can only seal what has been properly burned first, meaning anything that passes into the Frozen Seal position still carrying impurities will have those impurities locked into permanent form alongside everything else, which produces the kind of foundational instability that looks baffling from outside but is entirely predictable once you understand the sequence.

The practical consequences for anyone running experiments during ☉ at ♈ are significant and almost entirely ignored by working alchemists, who tend to attribute the results to instrument error or atmospheric conditions rather than the sky overhead. A carefully calibrated compound will shift during alignment if its reagents carry impurities, because the burning occurs regardless of experimental intent, and the result may end up purer than designed, which sounds like an improvement but often isn't when purity wasn't the goal of the experiment, or different enough from the intended composition to ruin the purpose entirely.

Ellie argued once that 🜍 operates similarly in that it identifies contradictions in pattern fields and erases them, and she wasn't wrong about how Xylos functions independently of cosmic position, but she had the mechanism inverted in a way that matters. Chaos fire forces erasure from outside the pattern, which is why it tends to be imprecise and why the results of chaos-purified material are less trustworthy than they appear, whereas Ash Forge reveals what should erase itself from within, which is why what remains afterward has a kind of integrity that externally purified material cannot quite achieve.
## Theralen and Xylos
♈ draws on ☉ and 🜍 in combination, which confounded every Academy theorist who tried to establish the eight schools as cleanly opposing forces, because holy illumination and chaotic dissolution operating as complements rather than opposites does not fit the categorical framework the Academy spent decades treating as settled theory. Theralen provides the clarity that makes impurities visible within the pattern field, and Xylos provides the dissolution that removes them once seen, and both are required because Theralen without Xylos produces only exposure without removal while Xylos without Theralen burns without discrimination, which is not purification in any meaningful sense and connects to no position on the ladder.

What ♈ begins, ♓ returns to complete, which sounds contradictory until you accept that the ladder has no true starting point and the burning that opens the cycle is the same burning that closes it from the other direction.
