# Construct 03: Liquid Fire
_Developed by Martin Ehrlich, 803 AD_  
_Recipe sourced from Venom Blade Alchemist_
## Purpose
To document the alchemical formula for a highly volatile incendiary compound ~~designed for deployment against afflicted entities~~. The substance exhibits spontaneous combustion upon exposure to ambient air, creating an effective area-denial weapon suitable for thrown deployment in sealed containers.
## Materials
### Primary Components
- **1 Part Venom Sap** (quantity varies by batch size)
  - Known for its warping properties when concentrated
  - Works synergistically with fire blossom to create ignition mechanism
  - Must be harvested fresh to maintain volatile properties

- **10 Parts Fire Blossom** (processed extract)
  - Contains dormant thermal energy in stable form
  - When exposed to venom sap's warping effects, releases fiery essence
  - Requires proper distillation to achieve optimal concentration

- **5 Parts Mutant Grease** (rendered and purified)
  - Serves as a powerful accelerant and primary fuel source
  - Transforms the ignition reaction into sustained, spreading flame
  - Provides the actual burning substance that adheres to targets
### Container Requirements
- **Sealed Vessel** (glass or ceramic preferred)
  - Must prevent air exposure during storage and transport
  - Designed for intentional breach upon impact
## Preparation Process
### Phase 1: Component Preparation
Venom sap must be extracted and maintained in oxygen-free environment to prevent degradation. Fire blossom requires careful distillation to concentrate active compounds without triggering combustion during processing.

Mutant grease undergoes rendering and purification to remove impurities that might interfere with combustion properties or cause unpredictable reactions with other components.
### Phase 2: Mixture Assembly
Removing oxygen from alchemical apparatuses is critical for success. To accomplish this, mutant grease is carefully boiled to create toxic fumes to fill glassware, expelling any air.

Combine components in precise ratios within sealed environment. The venom sap's warping properties interact with fire blossom extract to create the ignition mechanism. The sap destabilizes the blossom's contained thermal energy. Mutant grease is then incorporated as the accelerant and fuel source, transforming the reaction into actual burning substance.

**Critical Safety Note**: All mixing must occur in completely oxygen-free environment. The venom sap-fire blossom reaction becomes air-reactive once combined, and any air exposure will trigger immediate combustion amplified by the mutant grease accelerant.
### Phase 3: Container Sealing
Transfer completed mixture to deployment containers while maintaining air-tight seal. Containers must be designed for controlled breach upon impact while preventing accidental air exposure during storage or transport.
## Observed Properties
### Combustion Characteristics
Upon air exposure, the destabilized mixture ignites immediately. The mutant grease accelerant amplifies this reaction into intense, spreading flames that adhere to surfaces and burn for extended duration. The grease ensures continued combustion even on wet or treated surfaces, as its powerful accelerant properties override typical fire suppression methods.
### Storage Stability
When properly sealed, liquid fire maintains effectiveness indefinitely. No degradation of reactive properties observed in sealed samples after extended storage periods.
### Deployment Considerations
The substance is designed for thrown deployment in breakable containers. Impact breach allows immediate air contact, triggering combustion at point of delivery. Range and accuracy depend on container design and deployment method.
## Safety Warnings
- **Never expose to air during preparation or storage**
- **Handle only in controlled, oxygen-free environments**
- **Ensure proper container sealing before transport**
- **Deployment carries risk of collateral fire damage**
