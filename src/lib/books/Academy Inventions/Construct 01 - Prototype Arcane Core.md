# Construct 01: Prototype Arcane Core
_Developed by Martin Ehrlich, 802 AD_
## Purpose
To document the successful fabrication and initial testing of a composite magical storage device capable of containing multiple schools of magic simultaneously. The device utilizes pristine gemstones arranged in octahedral configuration, bound with Arcane Weave and encased in Onyx Tear material.

This design is a prototype and proof of concept. Further advancements to the design will be made.
## Materials
### Primary Components
- **Pristine Gemstones** (one per magical school)
  - Carved and shaped to form octahedral corner joints
  - Each gem corresponds to specific magical school affinity
  - Must maintain structural integrity throughout shaping process
### Binding Matrix
- **Arcane Weave** (fabrication process classified)
  - Serves as binding agent between gemstone components
  - Provides structural cohesion for octahedral assembly
  - Consumption occurs during final binding process
### Protective Casing
- **Onyx Tear Material** (master smith application required)
  - Wraps entire core assembly
  - Highly reactive to magical input
  - Application process requires specialized metalworking expertise
## Assembly Process
### Phase 1: Gemstone Preparation
Pristine gems were carved to precise specifications to achieve perfect octahedral corner configuration. Each gem was shaped to interface seamlessly with adjacent components while maintaining magical conductivity.

Base testing indicated optimal stability when gemstones were positioned with opposing magical schools directly across from each other within the octahedral structure. Final configuration utilized Blood opposite Illusion, Frost opposite Chaos, and Storm opposite Unholy.
### Phase 2: Weave Binding
Arcane Weave material was applied to create binding matrix between shaped gemstones. The Weave established structural cohesion, allowing the octahedral assembly to function as unified device.
### Phase 3: Onyx Encasement
Master smith application of Onyx Tear material provided protective casing around bound core. The material forms reactive outer layer capable of interfacing with external magical sources.
## Observed Properties
### Storage Capabilities
Initial testing confirmed successful multi-school magical storage within single device. Each gemstone component maintains its school-specific affinity while contributing to unified storage capacity.
### Unexpected Behavior
The completed Core demonstrated storage potential beyond design specifications. Initial experiments suggest the device may capture magical types it was not designed for, indicating broader compatibility than anticipated from the six-school configuration.
### Stability Assessment
Basic self-testing confirmed initial functionality without adverse effects. Subsequent stress testing involved subjecting the Core to significant magical saturation beyond normal operational parameters.

Under ambient magical flooding conditions, the device absorbed magic continuously until reaching apparent capacity threshold. At this point, absorption ceased entirely rather than continuing beyond safe limits. No structural failure or explosive discharge occurred during maximum saturation testing.

Current method for determining saturation level remains undetermined. Forced magical input beyond ambient absorption has not yet been attempted.

The Core maintains structural integrity under standard testing conditions. No degradation of binding matrix or casing material observed during preliminary trials.
