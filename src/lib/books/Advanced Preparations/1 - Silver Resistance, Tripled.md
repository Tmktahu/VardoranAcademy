# Advanced Preparations: Silver Resistance, Tripled
> **Series:** Advanced Preparations, Volume 1  
> **Author:** Malcolm Maxworthy, First Head of Chemical Studies  
> **Written:** AD 47
## How I Developed This Formula
After successfully creating Silver Resistance Brew, I faced obvious question: could we make it stronger? The brew worked well enough for brief silver exposure, but extended contact or combat against silver weapons required better protection.

The answer came from examining plague brier's silver resistance properties. Unlike snow flower's frost-based approach, plague brier provides silver protection through entirely different mechanism—probably related to its chemical contamination origins. I theorized that combining plague brier's chemical protection with proper preparation method might yield superior results.

Testing confirmed it. Plague brier alone, properly concentrated in glass bottle preparation, provides significantly stronger silver resistance than snow flower brew. The chemical rather than magical basis apparently creates more robust protection against silver's vampiric toxicity.

I spent four months refining concentration ratios before achieving consistent results. The breakthrough came from realizing glass bottles preserve plague brier's active compounds better than waterskins—the chemical properties degrade when exposed to organic materials.
## The Formula
**Ingredients:**
- 40 measures plague brier
- 1 empty glass bottle

**Preparation:**
Grind plague brier flowers thoroughly until achieving fine powder. The bright yellow petals should become uniform pale yellow dust. This requires more grinding than most plants—plague brier's structure resists pulverization.

Transfer ground material directly to glass bottle. Add clean water to fill. Seal immediately—plague brier's compounds begin degrading once exposed to air and water simultaneously. Shake vigorously for three minutes, much longer than standard preparations. The extended shaking ensures complete compound extraction.

Let steep minimum two hours. The potion turns murky yellow-brown and maintains slight chemical smell. This smell is normal and indicates proper preparation. Strain before use, though some sediment will remain suspended—that's expected with plague brier formulations.

**Effect:**
One bottle provides 60 minutes strong silver resistance. Vampiric users report ability to handle silver weapons briefly, tolerate silver-rich environments, and survive silver attacks that would otherwise cause severe harm. This is meaningful protection, not just tolerance.

Effect persists through death.
## Practical Uses
Combat against silver-wielding opponents benefits enormously from this potion. The enhanced protection allows vampiric warriors to engage enemies using silver weapons without constant retreat.

Researchers working with silver-based arcane implements consider this potion essential equipment. The protection enables extended handling of silver materials necessary for certain magical workings.

Merchants dealing in silver goods can conduct business normally with this protection. Extended exposure to silver currency, silver jewelry, silver decorative items—all become manageable rather than uncomfortable.

Some vampires use it when traveling through heavily-armed human territories where silver weapons are standard equipment. The protection provides peace of mind when surrounded by potential threats.
## Comparison with Basic Brew
Students ask whether this renders Silver Resistance Brew obsolete. The answer is nuanced:

**Protection Level:** This provides approximately three times the protection of basic brew. For serious silver exposure, this is clearly superior.

**Cost:** Plague brier is expensive and difficult to source. Snow flower is cheap and readily available. For minor protection, basic brew remains economical choice.

**Preparation:** This requires glass bottles and longer steeping time. Basic brew works with waterskins and minimal preparation. Field conditions favor simpler formulations.

**Source Material:** Plague brier grows exclusively in chemically contaminated regions. Snow flower grows widely across Vardoran. Availability affects practical accessibility.

Use basic brew for routine silver protection. Use this when facing serious silver threats or requiring extended exposure tolerance.
## Development Notes
**Attempt #11:** Tried combining plague brier with snow flower expecting additive effects. Result: the plants' different protection mechanisms interfered with each other, producing weaker protection than plague brier alone. Sometimes combinations reduce effectiveness rather than enhance it.

**Attempt #19:** Thought heating the mixture might accelerate compound extraction. Wrong. Heat destroyed plague brier's active compounds, creating completely inert liquid. Room temperature preparation only.

**Attempt #26:** Experimented with different concentration ratios. Found 40 measures optimal—more provides no additional benefit, less provides inadequate protection. The compound extraction reaches saturation at 40 measures in standard bottle.

**Attempt #33:** Tested adding fish bone as stabilizer like in power enhancement formulas. Accomplished nothing except making potion taste worse. Plague brier's chemical nature apparently doesn't require biological stabilization.
## Important Warnings
Store in glass bottles only. Plague brier's compounds react with certain organic materials, including leather and some woods. I learned this when stored batch ate through waterskin overnight.

The chemical smell is normal but shouldn't be overwhelming. If the potion smells intensely of chemicals rather than slightly, you've used contaminated plague brier or improper preparation. Discard and start over.

This is strong protection but not immunity. Extended silver contact will eventually overcome the resistance. Don't assume you can handle silver indefinitely just because you're protected.

Multiple doses within 60 minutes don't stack effects. The protection has ceiling—drinking more wastes expensive materials without increasing resistance.
