## Why Glass Bottles Matter
Glass provides several advantages over waterskins:

**Compound Preservation:** Glass is chemically inert. Won't react with preparation contents. Won't absorb active compounds. Won't leach organic materials into mixture. This matters significantly for concentrated formulations where purity affects potency.

**Pressure Tolerance:** Some preparations generate mild pressure during steeping. Waterskins bulge or leak. Glass bottles contain pressure safely assuming proper sealing.

**Visual Monitoring:** Transparent glass lets you observe color changes, precipitation, and other indicators of proper preparation. You can't see inside a waterskin, which creates guesswork about readiness.

**Temperature Stability:** Glass handles temperature variations better than organic materials. Important for formulations requiring specific temperature ranges during preparation.

Disadvantages exist too. Glass breaks. Glass is heavier. Glass costs more. But for intermediate and advanced work, the advantages outweigh drawbacks significantly.
## New Techniques You'll Learn
These eight formulations introduce several advanced concepts:

**Concentration Management:** Creating stronger effects by using more plant material per volume. Sounds simple. Actually requires careful ratio balancing because excessive concentration doesn't just increase potency; it can create instability or side effects.

**Sequential Resistance Building:** The Holy Resistance Flask demonstrates combining preparations to achieve effects impossible through single formulation. Understanding this principle opens doors to many advanced applications.

**Behavioral Effect Moderation:** Enhanced power formulas like Potion of Rage create stronger effects but also intensify side effects. Learning to work with rather than against these amplified properties separates competent alchemists from dangerous ones.

**Extended Steeping:** Glass bottle preparations often require longer extraction times than field brews. Patience becomes crucial skill. Rushing creates weak potions. Over-steeping degrades compounds.

## Safety Considerations (More Important Here)
Glass bottle preparations carry additional risks beyond field alchemy:

**Pressure Hazards:** Some formulations generate pressure during steeping. Always ensure proper sealing but don't over-tighten. Check bottles periodically for excessive pressure buildup. If a bottle feels unusually warm or shows stress cracks, carefully release pressure in controlled environment.

**Concentration Risks:** Higher potency means stronger side effects. Enhanced garlic resistance creates more aggression. Holy resistance causes more discomfort during preparation. Account for these intensified effects when working.

**Breakage Dangers:** Dropping glass bottle during preparation wastes expensive materials and creates cleanup problem. Spilled concentrated preparations can damage surfaces or create toxic environments depending on ingredients.

**Storage Requirements:** Glass bottles need proper storage. Stack carefully with padding between layers. Keep away from edge of shelves. Store away from temperature extremes.

Yes, I'm emphasizing safety more than Field Alchemy. That's because intermediate work carries genuine danger. Field brew mistakes create annoyance. Glass bottle preparation errors can create actual harm.
## Equipment Beyond Field Alchemy
You'll need additional tools:

**Glass Bottles:** Multiple high-quality bottles with proper seals. Inspect for cracks or imperfections before use. One bottle failure during critical preparation ruins your entire day.

**Better Grinding Equipment:** Larger mortar and pestle for processing 40-60 measures of material. Consider stone or ceramic variants that won't retain previous preparation residue.

**Sealing Materials:** Proper stoppers or caps for bottles. Cork works but can compress over time. Glass stoppers provide better seal.

**Temperature Monitoring:** Some preparations benefit from gentle warming. "Gentle" means barely noticeable, not actual heating. Learn the difference.

**Workspace Organization:** More complex preparations require more materials simultaneously available. Organize ingredients, tools, and containers systematically to prevent errors.

## Final Thoughts
Intermediate alchemy separates casual practitioners from serious alchemists. These formulations require dedication, patience, and willingness to fail repeatedly before succeeding. The enhanced effects justify the effort, but only if you're prepared to invest time and materials into mastering technique.

Don't be discouraged by initial failures. I destroyed countless batches developing these formulas. The difference is you get to learn from my failures plus your own, giving you advantage I lacked.

Start with Silver Resistance Potion because it builds directly on Silver Resistance Brew you already know. Progress through remaining formulas methodically. Take notes on your results. Learn systematically.
