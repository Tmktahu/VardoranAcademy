# Advanced Preparations: Blood Rose for Critical Cases
> **Series:** Advanced Preparations, Volume 5  
> **Author:** Malcolm Maxworthy, First Head of Chemical Studies  
> **Written:** AD 46
## How I Developed This Formula
Blood Rose Brew worked excellently for field medicine, but serious injuries required stronger healing. Combat wounds from powerful opponents, trauma from major accidents, life-threatening damage—all exceeded brew's healing capacity. I needed enhanced version for critical situations.

The solution involved increased concentration using glass bottle preparation. More blood rose per volume, longer steeping time, glass preservation of active compounds. The theory proved straightforward; execution required careful calibration.

I tested twelve different concentration ratios before finding optimal balance. Too little blood rose wastes expensive materials providing minimal improvement over basic brew. Too much creates suspension so thick it becomes difficult to consume while providing no additional healing benefit.

The 48-measure concentration in glass bottle format proved ideal. Provides approximately 40% more healing than basic brew while maintaining drinkable consistency and stable compound preservation.
## The Formula
**Ingredients:**
- 48 measures blood rose (fresh petals required for maximum potency)
- 1 empty glass bottle

**Preparation:**
Grind blood rose petals thoroughly until achieving very fine powder. The increased quantity requires longer grinding time. Consistency matters—uneven grinding produces uneven healing distribution.

Transfer ground material to glass bottle. Add clean water to fill, leaving minimal air space. Seal immediately. Shake vigorously for three minutes to ensure complete compound distribution. Let steep minimum one hour—longer than basic brew because higher concentration requires extended extraction time.

The potion turns very deep red, almost purple, and maintains strong sweet scent. The darker color indicates proper concentration. Strain before use, though thick suspension will remain.

**Effect:**
One bottle recovers approximately 55% of maximum health over 10 seconds. Like basic brew, healing works best out of combat—damage interrupts regeneration. This represents significantly stronger emergency healing.

The healing feels more intense than basic brew—stronger tingling sensation, faster tissue repair, more noticeable wound closure. Broken bones knit audibly, torn muscles reconnect visibly.
## Practical Uses
Critical injury treatment benefits most from enhanced healing. When wounds threaten life, this potion provides recovery speed that prevents fatality.

Elite military units carry these for serious combat situations. The extra healing often determines survival in battles against powerful opponents.

Dangerous expeditions into hostile territory justify carrying glass bottles despite fragility. The enhanced healing provides margin of safety when facing unknown threats.

Medical facilities stock these for emergency treatment. When patients arrive with life-threatening injuries, this potion stabilizes them until proper surgery can occur.
## Concentration Analysis
Testing revealed interesting properties of blood rose healing compounds:

**Linear Scaling:** Healing power increases proportionally with concentration up to approximately 50 measures. Beyond that, additional blood rose provides no benefit—the suspension reaches saturation.

**Glass Preservation:** Glass bottles preserve healing compounds approximately 30% better than waterskins. The organic materials in waterskins apparently absorb or degrade some compounds. Glass provides inert container maintaining full potency.

**Steeping Duration:** One hour steeping extracts roughly 90% of available healing compounds. Two hours reaches 95%. Beyond that, diminishing returns make extended steeping pointless.

**Fresh vs Dried:** Fresh blood rose petals provide approximately 25% more healing than dried specimens at identical concentrations. The drying process apparently damages some healing compounds despite preserving others.
## Comparison with Basic Brew
**Healing Power:** Approximately 40% stronger healing makes this clearly superior for serious injuries. For minor wounds, basic brew suffices. For life-threatening damage, use this.

**Cost:** Requires 50% more blood rose than basic brew. For routine healing, the additional cost may not justify modest improvement. For critical situations, cost becomes irrelevant.

**Container:** Glass bottles are heavier, more fragile than waterskins. Field conditions favor waterskins' durability. Medical facilities favor glass bottles' healing enhancement.

**Preparation Time:** Extended steeping makes rapid production impossible. Prepare in advance when possible. Basic brew works when immediate preparation is required.

Use basic brew for routine healing and field medicine. Use this when facing serious injury risk or treating critical wounds where maximum healing is essential.
## Important Warnings
The same combat limitations apply—healing interrupts when taking damage. Create safe moment for consumption or effect wastes.

The more intense healing can cause temporary disorientation. Your body processes rapid tissue repair while you're still conscious. This feels strange and can affect coordination briefly. Factor this into combat planning.

Glass bottles break more easily than waterskins. Carry carefully, pad adequately, check regularly for cracks. Broken bottle means lost expensive preparation.

Don't exceed two bottles within 10-minute period. Your body's healing compound processing has limits. Excessive consumption causes nausea and dizziness without providing additional healing benefit.
