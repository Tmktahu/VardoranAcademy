# Record 52: Stygian Crystal Formation – Arcane Weave Binding
> **Author:** Martin Ehrlich
> **Written:** 803 AD
## Subjects
- Arcane Weaves (2 units fabricated, 2 units previously available)
- Greater Stygian Shards (10,000 units total)
- Binding chamber apparatus
## Procedure Summary
Successful fabrication of two missing Weaves completed in the Arcane Department. Combined with two existing Weaves, all four units were used in binding protocol with Greater Stygian Shards as primary material.

Initial binding commenced with 1,000 Greater Stygian Shards. The Weaves formed a barrier hovering in the central workspace... creating a controlled environment for material binding.
## Observations
### Initial Material Processing
Stygian Shards began immediate arrangement and compression.
### Fracture and Realignment Process
Each shard fractured and then realigned. The process resulted in progressive compression into a compact, amorphous mass.

Additional crystal fragments added to the system were broken down and incorporated into the primary structure. Secondary fragments formed an orbiting ring of smaller particles around the central mass.
### Material Density Assessment
Progressive addition of shards resulted in increasingly defined structure. Completion of the experiment occurred when all 10,000 Stygian Shards were added... filling the space within the barrier.
### Crystal Development
Each added shard followed the same fracture-realignment cycle... creating a consistent pattern of breakdown and reconstruction. The emerging structure showed clear similarity to crystals documented in Mortium for greater Stygian rift activation.
## Final Product Characteristics
### Physical Properties
- Well-defined structure... essentially a large constructed Stygian crystal
- Very reminiscent of crystals found in Mortium
- Produces audible humming at significant volume
- Demonstrates spatial distortion effects in immediate vicinity
- Safe for manual handling under current protocols
### Formation Completion
The Weave material was consumed during final formation as the crystal achieved completion.

The crystal descended beneath the aperture and rose again, completing the synthesis process successfully.
## Current Status
The completed crystal is housed in Zrott's laboratory for ongoing investigation and experimentation. The object appears stable and poses no immediate hazard under controlled conditions.

Movement of the crystal requires caution due to unknown spatial distortion properties and potential interaction with local magical infrastructure.
## Access Protocol
Gilded Compass members may request supervised experimentation with the crystal. All testing must be conducted within Zrott's laboratory premises until transport safety protocols are established.
